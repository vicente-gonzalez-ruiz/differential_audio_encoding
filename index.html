1 Motivation

  • Usually happens that the diﬀerences between consecutive PCM (Pulse-Code Modulation) [2, 3] samples
    e[n] = s[n] − s[n − 1],

    tend to have a smaller variance (and therefore, smaller entropy) than the original (s) ones

    σ2(e) ≤ σ2(s),

    which potentially provides better lossless compression ratios for e than for s.

  • Finally, notice that by deﬁnition, if a source of “noise” (such as the quantization noise) has not been introduced during the encoding process, diﬀerential encoding is a fully reversible process:
    s[n] = e[n] + s[n − 1].

3 DPCM (Diﬀerential PCM)

  • Signals sampled at Nyquist rate exhibit correlation between consecutive samples. Therefore, the variance of the ﬁrst diﬀerence
    σ2({s[n + 1] − s[n]} n=0N−1) (1)

    will be smaller than the variance of the signal itself

    σ2({s[n + 1] − s[n]} n=0N−1) < σ2(s) = σ s2. (2)
  • In general, DPCM consist in computing the residual (error signal):
    e[n] = s[n] −ŝ[n] (3)

    where for the simplest case:

    ŝ[n] = s[n − 1] (4)
  • Used in the G.726 standard.

4 ADPCM (Adaptive DPCM)

  • In orter to minimize σe2, the predictor P can change its behaviour depending on the characteristics of s.

5 Forward adaptive prediction

  • Splits the input into blocks, optimizes the predictor for each block and sends with each ADPCM block the predictor coeﬃcients as side information.

6 Optimal prediction based on Weiner-Hopf optimization

  • The predictor can be any structure capable of producing a signal
    ŝ ≈ s. (5)
  • In the case of using LPC (Linear Predictive Coding), where
    ŝ[n] = ∑ i=1Ma is[n − i], (6)

    {ai}i=1M are the LPC coeﬃcients and M is the predictor order.

  • Optimal foeﬀs {ai} can be found when they minimize the variance of the residue (error signal)
    σe2 = E(s[n] −∑ i=1Ma is[n − i])2. (7)
  • For this, it must me hold that
    ∂σe2 ∂a1 = −2Es[n] −∑ i=1Ma is[n − i]s[n − 1] = 0 ∂σe2 ∂a2 = −2Es[n] −∑ i=1Ma is[n − i]s[n − 2 = 0 ⋮ ∂σe2 ∂aM = −2Es[n] −∑ i=1Ma is[n − i]s[n − M] = 0 . (8)
  • Appying expectations we get that
    ∑ i=1Ma iRss(i − 1) = Rss(1) ∑ i=1Ma iRss(i − 2) = Rss(2) ⋮ ∑ i=1Ma iRss(i − M) = Rss(M) , (Weiner-Hopf equations)

    where Rss(k) is the autorrelation function of s, deﬁned as

    Rss(k) = Es[n]s[n − k] = 1 M − k∑ i=1M−ks[i]s[i + k]. (autocorrelation)

8 Backward adaptive prediction

  • Both, the encoder and the decoder can constinuosly adapt the prediction to the characteristics of s, if both only use the information that is available at the decoder.

9 Adaptive prediction based on Least Mean Squared (LMS) algorithm

  • As we know,
    e[n] = s[n] −ŝ[n] = s[n] −∑ i=1Ma is[n − i]. (9)
  • Our objective is to minimize e[n], which is the same that minimizing the energy of the prediction error e2, by controlling the {ai}i=1M coeﬃcients.
  • Suppose that we can control iteratively these coeﬃcients, by incrementing or decrementing them with a proportionality constant α (the larger α, the faster the convergence but possiblely the oscillation, and viceversa). Let’s denote ai[n] the value of coeﬀ ai at iteration n of the algorithm. If happens, for example, that
    ∂e2[n] ∂ai < 0 (10)

    (the squared prediction error for iteration n has been increased compared to the previous iteration), then the prediction ŝ[n] should have been larger, and viceversa. Let’s deﬁne the iterative control of {ai}i=1M coeﬃcients as

    ai[n+1] = a i[n] − α∂e2[n] ∂ai i=1M (LMS_idea)
  • Applying derivatives we obtain for ai that
    ∂e2[n] ∂ai = ∂s[n] −∑ i=1Mais[n − i]2 ∂ai = −2s[n]−∑ i=1Ma is[n−i]s[n−i] = −2e[n]s[n−i]. (11)
  • Substituting this expression in Eq. (LMS_idea), we get that
    ai[n+1] = a i[n] + 2αe[n]s[n − i] i=1M. (LMS_control)
  • Notice that, in a backward adaptive prediction scheme, both elements e[n] and s[n − i] are known at iteration n at both, the encoder and the decoder.

10 Lossy DPCM

  • The prediction error is quantized to decrease the variance
    ẽ[n] = Q(e[n]), (12)

    where

    e[n] = s[n] −s̃̂[n], (13)

    where

    s̃[n] = s̃̂[n] + ẽ[n]. (14)
  • Used in hybrid (lossy DPCM/transform) video coding [1].
